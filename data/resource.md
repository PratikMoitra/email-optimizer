## What Makes Reply Rate Go Up

### Lead Quality (highest leverage)
- Tighter job title filter = higher relevance = higher reply rate
- Best titles: CEO, Founder, Owner, Managing Director (decision-makers)
- Avoid: VP, Director at 500+ person companies (gatekept by layers)
- Company size sweet spot: 5-50 employees (big enough to need your service, small enough that the founder reads email)
- Industry matters: agencies, consulting firms, and service businesses reply 2-3x more than SaaS or ecommerce

### Subject Lines
- Lowercase performs better than Title Case in cold email
- Under 40 characters
- Personal > clever (e.g., "quick question" > "Revolutionize Your Operations")
- Question format adds ~15% open lift
- "re:" tricks work short-term but hurt domain reputation — avoid

### Body Copy
- First line must not be about you — reference something about THEM
- Keep under 100 words total
- One CTA only — "quick call" or "worth a chat" (not "book a demo")
- No em dashes, no corporate jargon, no exclamation marks
- Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences (mobile reading)
- Use {{firstName}} and {{companyName}} merge tags

### Follow-ups
- Single step only (Step 1, Day 0) — no follow-up sequences
- All signal comes from the initial email; follow-ups dilute measurement

### Deliverability
- text_only or minimal HTML (no images, no heavy formatting)
- No link tracking (hurts deliverability)
- No open tracking (same reason)
- Keep daily limit at 125/campaign max
- email_gap of 10+ minutes between sends

## Mutation Strategy
Levers to pull (highest leverage first):
1. Lead filter (job_titles, company_keywords, industry, size) — different audience
2. Subject line — different hook
3. Opening line — different personalization angle
4. CTA — different ask (call vs audit vs case study)
5. Body length — shorter vs longer
6. Tone — casual vs professional

Early experiments (no history): go bold, change multiple categories at once to maximize search space.
Later experiments (with history): make targeted changes based on winning patterns.

## What NOT to Mutate (Hold Constant)
- email_gap (10 min)
- daily_limit (125/campaign)
- Number of steps (1 — single email only)
- Sending schedule (9am-5pm CT, all days)
- link_tracking: off
- open_tracking: off
